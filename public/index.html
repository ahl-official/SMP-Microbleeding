Procedure Risks

Specific to SMP and Microblading

Microblading: Fine needles deposit pigment into the upper dermis. Minor skin penetration and mild bleeding is normal. Pigment may fade unevenly. Colour appears darker immediately and lightens by 30–50% during healing.
Scalp Micropigmentation (SMP): Specialised equipment implants pigment dots resembling hair follicles. Results vary by skin type, oiliness, and sun exposure. Multiple sessions may be required for optimal coverage.
General Risks: Infection if aftercare is not followed, temporary redness or swelling, uneven fading, need for touch-up sessions, and in rare cases — allergic reaction to pigment.
💊

Aftercare Instructions

Strict adherence is essential for best results

Avoid direct sunlight, swimming, and sweating for at least 10 days post-procedure.

Do not wet, pick, or scratch the treated area during healing (7–14 days).

Apply the provided aftercare balm as instructed — keep the area clean and moisturised.

After healing, always apply SPF 30+ sunscreen to protect pigment longevity.

A touch-up session is typically required 4–8 weeks after for optimal results.

Contact us immediately if you notice increased redness, swelling, pus, or fever.
